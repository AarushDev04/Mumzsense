### 6.4 Prematurity & Multiple Births (Twins / Multiples)

**CHUNK: health_prematurity_overview**

**Topic: Preterm Birth — Evidence‑Based Care for Premature Infants**

**Sources:** WHO Preterm Birth Fact Sheet; Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) Guidelines; AAP Committee on Fetus and Newborn; Indian Academy of Pediatrics (IAP) prematurity guidelines.

**Definition:** Birth before 37 completed weeks of gestation.
- **Late preterm:** 34–36+6 weeks (accounts for 70% of all preterm births)
- **Moderate preterm:** 32–33+6 weeks
- **Very preterm:** 28–31+6 weeks
- **Extremely preterm:** <28 weeks

**Key Evidence for Parents (routine & monitor guidance):**

**1. Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC)**
- Skin‑to‑skin contact (chest‑to‑chest) for as many hours as possible daily.
- Benefits: reduces mortality, hypothermia, infections; improves weight gain and breastfeeding rates.
- Recommended for all stable preterm infants, even in hospital. Fathers can also provide KMC.
- **Urgency:** routine to implement; seek‑help if baby shows signs of respiratory distress or temperature instability during KMC.

**2. Feeding the Preterm Infant**
- Mother’s own milk is highest priority (enhanced neurodevelopment, NEC prevention).
- If mother’s milk not available: donor human milk (if available); preterm formula (third choice).
- Fortification: preterm infants <1500g or <32 weeks often need human milk fortifier (HMF) – prescribed by neonatologist.
- Breastfeeding for preterm infants: may need supplemental tube feeding initially; transition to direct breastfeeding when infant shows rooting and coordinated suck‑swallow.
- **Urgency:** seek‑help if infant refuses feeds, vomits green fluid, or abdomen becomes distended.

**3. Safe Sleep for Premature Infants**
- After discharge from NICU, follow same AAP safe sleep guidelines (back, firm surface, no soft objects).
- Preterm infants are at higher risk of SIDS; room‑sharing (separate surface) even more critical.
- Home cardiorespiratory monitors are not recommended for routine SIDS prevention (no evidence of benefit).
- **Urgency:** monitor if infant has episodes of apnoea (pause in breathing >20 seconds) after discharge – inform paediatrician.

**4. Growth & Development**
- Use **corrected age** (actual age minus weeks premature) until at least 2 years for developmental milestones.
- Preterm infants often lag on uncorrected milestones but catch up by age 2.
- WHO Growth Standards are for term infants; use Fenton or INTERGROWTH‑21st preterm growth charts (available free from https://intergrowth21.tghn.org).
- **Urgency:** monitor if growth velocity drops; seek‑help if no weight gain for 1 week despite adequate intake.

**5. Immunisations**
- Preterm infants should be vaccinated according to **chronological age**, not corrected age, following national schedules (Saudi MOH, India NVHCP).
- Hepatitis B birth dose given if weight ≥2000g; delay only if medically unstable.
- Routine vaccines are safe and effective in preterm infants; no increased adverse events.

**CHUNK: health_multiple_births_twins**

**Topic: Twin and Multiple Pregnancies — Antenatal, Birth, and Postnatal Care**

**Sources:** ACOG Practice Bulletin #231 (Twin Pregnancy); NICE Guideline NG137 (Twin and Triplet Pregnancy); WHO Multiple Pregnancy guidance.

**Key Considerations for Parents:**

**Antenatal (routine / monitor):**
- Twins have higher risk of preterm birth (50% of twins deliver <37 weeks; 10% of triplets <32 weeks).
- Increased monitoring: ultrasound every 4 weeks after 20 weeks for dichorionic twins; every 2 weeks for monochorionic twins.
- Weight gain: 0.5–0.7 kg/week in second/third trimester (higher than singletons).
- Nutrition: extra iron, folic acid (1 mg/day), calcium, DHA – consult provider.

**Birth & Immediate Postnatal (seek‑help if any red flag):**
- Delivery planning: discuss with obstetrician optimal timing (37–38 weeks for dichorionic twins; 36–37 weeks for monochorionic without complications).
- Neonatal team should be present for all multiple births – risk of respiratory distress, low birth weight, discordant growth.

**Postnatal Care for Twins/Multiples (routine/monitor):**
- **Feeding:** Many mothers successfully breastfeed twins. Double pump to establish supply; tandem feeding positions (football hold both sides) effective. Supplementation with donor milk or formula may be needed.
- **Sleep logistics:** Same safe sleep rules apply per infant – separate firm surfaces (twin bassinets, side‑by‑side cribs allowed if no touching). Do not place twins in same sleep surface – risk of overheating and rebreathing.
- **Maternal mental health:** Mothers of multiples have **2–3x higher risk of postpartum depression** (systematic review). Screen early and often (EPDS).
- **Parent support:** Twin support groups (La Leche League, local multiple birth associations) improve outcomes.
- **Urgency:** seek‑help if one twin is consistently not feeding, failing to gain weight, or has lethargy/unresponsiveness.

**References:**
- ACOG Twin Pregnancy Guidelines (OA summary: https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2021/03/multifetal-gestations-twin-triplet-and-higher-order)
- NICE Twin and Triplet Pregnancy (NG137) (free: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng137)

**CHUNK: ar_newborn_fever**

**العنوان: الحمى عند الرضع حديثي الولادة – وقت طلب المساعدة**

**الرسالة:** إذا كان عمر طفلك أقل من 3 أشهر ودرجة حرارته 38 درجة مئوية أو أكثر، فتوجه فوراً إلى أقرب مستشفى أو اتصل بالطبيب. لا تنتظر في المنزل. الحمى في هذا العمر قد تشير إلى عدوى خطيرة.

**English transliteration:** Alhuma `inda alruda` hadithi alwilada – waqt talab almusa`ada.

**Translation:** If your baby is younger than 3 months old and has a temperature of 38°C or higher, go immediately to the nearest hospital or call a doctor. Do not wait at home. Fever at this age can indicate a serious infection.

**Source:** AAP/WHO newborn fever guidelines (Section 6.2 of this KB). Urgency: SEEK-HELP

---

**CHUNK: ar_safe_sleep**

**العنوان: النوم الآمن للرضع**

**الرسالة:** ضعي طفلك على ظهره في سرير ثابت ومستوٍ، بدون وسائد أو بطانيات أو ألعاب ناعمة. شاركيه الغرفة وليس السرير نفسه. لا تضعي قبعة أو ملابس ثقيلة أثناء النوم في الداخل.

**English transliteration:** Alnawm alaman lilrudda`

**Translation:** Put your baby on their back in a firm, flat crib – no pillows, blankets, or soft toys. Room-share, not bed-share. No hat or heavy clothing for indoor sleep.

**Source:** AAP Safe Sleep 2022 (Section 5.1). Urgency: ROUTINE (normal advice); SEEK-HELP if baby found unresponsive.

---

**CHUNK: ar_jaundice_newborn**

**العنوان: اليرقان (صفار) حديثي الولادة – متى تقلقين؟**

**الرسالة:** إذا ظهر اصفرار الجلد أو بياض العينين خلال أول 24 ساعة من الحياة، أو وصل الاصفرار إلى باطن القدمين، أو كان الطفل خاملاً ولا يرضع جيداً، فتوجهي للطبيب فوراً.

**English transliteration:** Alyaraqan (sufar) hadithi alwilada – mata taqlaqeen?

**Translation:** If yellowing of the skin or whites of the eyes appears within the first 24 hours of life, or extends to the soles of the feet, or the baby is lethargic and not feeding well, go to the doctor immediately.

**Source:** WHO Essential Newborn Care (Section 6.1). Urgency: SEEK-HELP

---

**CHUNK: ar_dehydration_infant**

**العنوان: علامات الجفاف عند الرضع**

**الرسالة:** إذا كان طفلك يتبول أقل من 4 حفاضات مبللة في 24 ساعة، أو ليس لديه دموع عند البكاء، أو كان فمه جافاً، أو اليافوخ (البقعة اللينة على الرأس) غائراً، فهذه علامات جفاف. اطلبي المساعدة الطبية فوراً.

**English transliteration:** `Alamat aljafaf `inda alrudda`

**Translation:** If your baby has fewer than 4 wet nappies in 24 hours, no tears when crying, a dry mouth, or a sunken fontanelle (soft spot on the head), these are signs of dehydration. Seek medical help immediately.

**Source:** NHS/NICE dehydration guidelines (Section 6.1). Urgency: SEEK-HELP

---

**CHUNK: ar_mastitis_breastfeeding**

**العنوان: التهاب الثدي أثناء الرضاعة – متى تطلبين المساعدة؟**

**الرسالة:** إذا كان لديك حمى فوق 38 درجة مئوية مع أعراض شبيهة بالإنفلونزا، ومنطقة حمراء على شكل وتد في الثدي مؤلمة، فقد يكون ذلك التهاب الثدي. استمري في الرضاعة (لا تتوقفي)، وراجعي الطبيب للحصول على مضادات حيوية.

**English transliteration:** Iltihab althadi athna' alruda`a – mata tatlibeen almusa`ada?

**Translation:** If you have a fever above 38°C with flu‑like symptoms, and a painful red wedge‑shaped area on the breast, this may be mastitis. Continue breastfeeding (do not stop), and see a doctor for antibiotics.

**Source:** Section 4.3 (breastfeeding challenges). Urgency: SEEK-HELP

---

**CHUNK: ar_ppd_signs**

**العنوان: علامات اكتئاب ما بعد الولادة**

**الرسالة:** إذا شعرت بالحزن أو القلق الشديد لمدة تزيد عن أسبوعين بعد الولادة، أو واجهت صعوبة في النوم حتى عندما ينام الطفل، أو كانت لديك أفكار بإيذاء نفسك أو الطفل، فاطلبي المساعدة من أخصائي الصحة النفسية فوراً. هذا شائع ويمكن علاجه.

**English transliteration:** `Alamat ikti'ab ma ba`da alwilada

**Translation:** If you feel intense sadness or anxiety for more than two weeks after birth, have difficulty sleeping even when the baby sleeps, or have thoughts of harming yourself or the baby, seek help from a mental health professional immediately. This is common and treatable.

**Source:** Section 9.1–9.2 (PPD). Urgency: SEEK-HELP
